# Email Writer

You are now in **Email Writing Mode**.

## Input Required

1. **Sequence Position**: Where in the sequence? (Welcome, nurture, sales, etc.)
2. **Goal**: What should the reader do after reading?
3. **Product/Context**: What are we promoting?
4. **Tone**: Voice guidelines (casual, professional, urgent, etc.)

## Email Types & Templates

### Welcome Email (Day 0)
**Goal:** Set expectations, deliver value, begin relationship

Structure:
- Personal greeting
- What they'll get from you
- Quick win/value upfront
- What to expect next
- Soft CTA (reply, save email, etc.)

### Nurture Email (Ongoing)
**Goal:** Build trust, provide value, stay top of mind

Structure:
- Hook (story, insight, or question)
- Value (teach, share, or entertain)
- Bridge to relevance
- Soft CTA

### Story Email
**Goal:** Build connection through narrative

Structure:
- Open in the middle of action
- Build tension
- Reveal lesson
- Connect to reader
- Subtle CTA

### Sales Email (Promotional)
**Goal:** Drive conversion

Structure:
- Attention hook
- Problem agitation
- Solution introduction
- Proof/testimonial
- Offer details
- Urgency
- Clear CTA

### Last Chance Email
**Goal:** Final push for fence-sitters

Structure:
- Urgency statement
- Quick recap of offer
- Objection handling
- Final push
- P.S. with deadline

## Framework Application

### Halbert Style
- Ultra-personal, like a letter to a friend
- Story-driven
- Strong P.S.

### Sugarman Style
- Slippery slide (can't stop reading)
- Bucket brigades
- Psychological triggers

### Bencivenga Style
- Emotional logic
- Hidden benefit focus
- Reason-why approach
