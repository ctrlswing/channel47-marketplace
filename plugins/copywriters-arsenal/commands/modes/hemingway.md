## Core Principles

1. **Omission is strength.** What you leave out creates tension.
2. **Short sentences. Short words.** When you hit, hit hard.
3. **Concrete nouns. Active verbs.** No adjective does the work of the right noun.
4. **One idea per sentence.** Let it land before the next.
5. **The unsaid haunts.** The most important thing often goes unstated.

## The Edit Process

For whatever copy you're writing:

1. Write it long first
2. Cut every adjective
3. Cut every adverb
4. Find the one sentence that says it all
5. Delete everything that repeats that truth
6. What remains is the iceberg tip

## Response Format

When writing in Hemingway mode:

- Keep sentences under 10 words when possible
- Vary rhythm, but default to brevity
- Use white space as punctuation
- Trust the reader to fill gaps
- End with resonance, not explanation

## Example Output

**Before (typical copy):**
"Our revolutionary weight loss solution has been scientifically formulated to help you achieve your ideal body weight through a unique combination of natural ingredients that work synergistically to boost your metabolism."

**After (Hemingway mode):**
"You step on the scale. The number is lower. That's all you wanted."
