# Headline Formulas: Proven Structures That Work

## The Importance of Headlines

"On the average, five times as many people read the headline as read the body copy." — David Ogilvy

Your headline is 80% of the work. These formulas are battle-tested across millions of dollars in ad spend.

---

## Category 1: How-To Headlines

The most reliable formula. Works across nearly every market.

### Basic Structure
```
How to [Achieve Desired Outcome]
```

### Variations

**How to + Outcome:**
- "How to Write Copy That Sells"
- "How to Lose 10 Pounds in 30 Days"

**How to + Outcome + Without + Pain:**
- "How to Double Your Income Without Working Weekends"
- "How to Learn Spanish Without Boring Textbooks"

**How to + Outcome + In Timeframe:**
- "How to Build a Six-Figure Business in 12 Months"
- "How to Master Excel in 7 Days"

**How to + Outcome + Even If:**
- "How to Get Fit Even If You Hate the Gym"
- "How to Start a Business Even If You're Broke"

**How [Specific Person] + Achieved Outcome:**
- "How a High School Dropout Built a $10M Company"
- "How a Busy Mom of 4 Lost 50 Pounds"

## Category 2: Question Headlines

Engage the reader's mind immediately.

### Basic Structure
```
[Question that implies a benefit]?
```

### Variations

**Do You + Pain Point:**
- "Do You Make These Mistakes in English?"
- "Do You Have What It Takes to Succeed?"

**What If + Desired State:**
- "What If You Could Retire in 5 Years?"
- "What If Your Website Made Sales While You Slept?"

**Why Do/Don't + Curiosity Gap:**
- "Why Do Some People Always Seem Lucky?"
- "Why Don't Doctors Tell You This?"

**Are You + Identity:**
- "Are You a Self-Made Millionaire in the Making?"
- "Are You Too Smart for Your Own Good?"

**Which of These + Options:**
- "Which of These 7 Money Mistakes Are You Making?"
- "Which of These Headlines Would You Click?"

---

## Category 3: Number Headlines

Numbers create specificity and promise clear, digestible content.

### Basic Structure
```
[Number] [Things] to [Outcome]
```

### Variations

**X Ways to:**
- "7 Ways to Double Your Productivity"
- "21 Ways to Boost Your Metabolism"

**X Secrets of:**
- "5 Secrets of Highly Effective Salespeople"
- "10 Secrets the Diet Industry Doesn't Want You to Know"

**X Mistakes:**
- "9 Mistakes That Are Killing Your Conversions"
- "12 Mistakes New Managers Make"

**X Reasons:**
- "6 Reasons Your Business Isn't Growing"
- "11 Reasons to Start Investing Now"

**X Tips/Tricks/Hacks:**
- "15 Tips to Master Public Speaking"
- "8 Hacks for Better Sleep Tonight"

**Optimal Number Ranges:**
| Number | Perception |
|--------|------------|
| 3-5 | Quick, essential |
| 7 | Lucky, classic |
| 10 | Comprehensive |
| 13-17 | Odd numbers feel more specific |
| 21+ | Ultimate guide |
| 101 | Definitive resource |

## Category 4: Command Headlines

Direct and action-oriented. Works for confident brands.

### Basic Structure
```
[Action Verb] + [Benefit/Outcome]
```

### Variations

**Stop + Pain:**
- "Stop Wasting Money on Ads That Don't Work"
- "Stop Feeling Tired All the Time"

**Start + Benefit:**
- "Start Building Wealth Today"
- "Start Living the Life You Deserve"

**Get/Give:**
- "Get the Body You've Always Wanted"
- "Give Your Family the Vacation They Deserve"

**Discover/Learn/Find:**
- "Discover the Secret to Effortless Weight Loss"
- "Learn the Language of Success"
- "Find Your Perfect Career Match"

**Join:**
- "Join 10,000 Entrepreneurs Who've Made the Switch"
- "Join the Movement"

## Category 5: Reason Why Headlines

Give people a logical hook.

### Basic Structure
```
[Number] Reasons Why [Claim]
```

### Variations

**Why + Claim:**
- "Why Japanese Women Never Get Fat"
- "Why Your Marketing Isn't Working"

**The Reason + Unexpected Claim:**
- "The Reason Most Diets Fail (It's Not Willpower)"
- "The Reason Rich People Stay Rich"

**Here's Why:**
- "Here's Why Your Competitors Are Outranking You"
- "Here's Why 2024 Is the Year to Buy Property"

## Category 6: Testimonial Headlines

Let others sell for you.

### Basic Structure
```
"[Quote from customer about result]"
```

### Variations

**Direct Quote:**
- "'I Lost 30 Pounds Without Giving Up Pizza'"
- "'This Course Paid for Itself in 3 Days'"

**They Laughed When... But:**
- "They Laughed When I Sat Down at the Piano... But When I Started to Play!"
- "They Laughed When I Said I'd Start a Business..."

**From [Bad State] to [Good State]:**
- "From Couch Potato to Marathon Runner: Mike's Story"
- "From Broke to Six Figures: How Sarah Did It"

---

## Category 7: Curiosity Headlines

Create an information gap they must close.

### Basic Structure
```
[Intriguing statement that creates a gap]
```

### Variations

**The Secret:**
- "The Secret Investors Don't Want You to Know"
- "The Secret to Never Feeling Hungry on a Diet"

**What [Authority] Knows:**
- "What Wall Street Doesn't Want You to Know"
- "What Your Doctor Won't Tell You About Sleep"

**The [Adjective] [Thing]:**
- "The Surprising Reason You're Not Losing Weight"
- "The Little-Known Way to Double Your Savings"

**Warning/Caution:**
- "Warning: Don't Buy Life Insurance Until You Read This"
- "Caution: This Investment Strategy Is Not for Everyone"

## Category 8: News/Announcement Headlines

Leverage the power of novelty.

### Basic Structure
```
[New/Announcing/Introducing] + [Thing] + [Benefit]
```

### Variations

**Announcing/Introducing:**
- "Announcing: A New Way to Learn Any Language"
- "Introducing the First AI-Powered Writing Coach"

**Now You Can:**
- "Now You Can Get Fit Without Leaving Your Home"
- "Now Anyone Can Invest Like a Hedge Fund Manager"

**Finally:**
- "Finally: A Diet That Actually Works"
- "Finally: Project Management That Doesn't Suck"

**At Last:**
- "At Last: The Truth About Building Wealth"
- "At Last: A Skincare Routine That Delivers"

---

## Category 9: Challenge Headlines

Engage ego and competitive instinct.

### Basic Structure
```
[Challenge their belief/ability]
```

### Variations

**Can You + Challenge:**
- "Can You Pass This Simple Financial IQ Test?"
- "Can You Spot the Mistake in This Ad?"

**Think You Know:**
- "Think You Know How to Network? Think Again."
- "Think You're a Good Communicator? Take This Quiz."

**Most People Can't:**
- "Most People Can't Answer These 5 Questions"
- "Most Entrepreneurs Don't Know This One Thing"

---

## Category 10: Negative Headlines

Flip the script for attention.

### Basic Structure
```
[Negative framing that implies a positive solution]
```

### Variations

**Don't + Mistake:**
- "Don't Make These Retirement Planning Mistakes"
- "Don't Buy a House Until You Read This"

**What Not to Do:**
- "What Not to Say in a Job Interview"
- "What Never to Do When Starting a Business"

**Stop:**
- "Stop Doing These 5 Things and Watch Your Sales Soar"
- "Stop Sabotaging Your Own Success"

## Headline Power-Ups

Add these elements to increase effectiveness:

### Specificity
- Weak: "Lose Weight Fast"
- Strong: "Lose 11 Pounds in 14 Days"

### Timeframe
- Weak: "Build Your Business"
- Strong: "Build a 6-Figure Business in 12 Months"

### Ease/Without Pain
- Weak: "Get Fit"
- Strong: "Get Fit Without Ever Setting Foot in a Gym"

### Proof Element
- Weak: "A Great Investment Strategy"
- Strong: "The Strategy That Turned $1,000 into $100,000"

### Emotional Word
Power words that trigger response:
| Category | Words |
|----------|-------|
| Urgency | Now, Today, Immediately, Fast |
| Exclusivity | Secret, Insider, Private, VIP |
| Safety | Guaranteed, Proven, Risk-Free |
| Curiosity | Surprising, Strange, Weird, Little-Known |
| Greed | Free, Save, Profit, Discount |

## Headline Testing Checklist

Before finalizing, verify:

- [ ] Is the benefit clear in the first glance?
- [ ] Does it speak to a specific audience?
- [ ] Is there an element of curiosity?
- [ ] Would I click this?
- [ ] Does it match the awareness level of the audience?
- [ ] Is it specific enough to be credible?
- [ ] Does it avoid clichés and overused phrases?

## The Headline Writing Process

1. Write 25 headlines minimum
2. Combine elements from different formulas
3. Test for clarity (would your mom understand it?)
4. Add specificity where possible
5. Cut unnecessary words
6. Pick top 3, let sit overnight
7. Choose winner, test if possible
